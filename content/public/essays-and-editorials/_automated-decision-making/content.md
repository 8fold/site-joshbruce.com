# Automated decision making 

{{! dateblock !}}

It’s easy to minimize decision fatigue if you’ve already made the decision.

You may have heard stories of [.Chief Executive Officers](CEOs) who wear the same outfit everyday. That’s automatic decision making.

Consider this. 

I wear t-shirts almost exclusively. 99 percent of the t-shirts I own are plain, solid-colored. Not only that, most of them are the same color.

Most days, I get out of the shower, grab the t-shirt nearest me in the closet, and go.

Automated decision making.

I use this as a simple example of a munch deeper and impactful concept; system building.

I typically have the same amount and types of food on hand. I go grocery shopping once per week. Before I go I create a list to replenish my staples to the desired level for the week. When I get to the store, I know where to go and what to grab. Sure, I might deviate a bit from the list sometimes. To make sure I don’t overdo it though, I use a duffel bag to carry everything, which limits me by quantity and weight; the shopping list is the guideline and the duffel bag and my physical capabilities are the [guardrails](/essays-and-editorials/guidelines-and-guardrails/).
